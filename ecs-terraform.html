The Pain Points: Before Cloud Migration

ShopVerse’s growing eCommerce platform, built on PHP/Node.js, was constrained by a legacy VM setup, leading to frequent outages during peak sales and high operational overhead.

Slow, Manual Scaling

Scaling required support tickets and hours of waiting. Outages occurred 2-3 times per quarter due to capacity limits.

Config Drift & Errors

Environments (Dev/Staging/Prod) never matched, leading to "works on my machine" issues and config incompatibilities during manual rollbacks.

High MTTR (90m)

Distributed logs and lack of centralized visibility meant Mean Time To Recovery was often 90 minutes or more.

Inconsistent Costs

Bills were unpredictable, fluctuating wildly from over-provisioning during peak emergencies to under-provisioning afterward.

Migration Options Considered

We evaluated three pathways against the client's goals for speed, cost, and maintainability.

1. Lift-and-Shift (EC2)

Migrate VMs as-is.

  • Pros: Quick (2-3 months).
  • Cons: No real scaling improvement. Manual operations remain.
  • Decision: Rejected - Failed to solve core scaling issues.
2. Full Kubernetes (EKS)

Containerize + Managed K8s.

  • Pros: Ultimate flexibility/microservices readiness.
  • Cons: High cost (20%+ more), steep learning curve, slow start (4-5 months).
  • Decision: Rejected - Too complex and costly for the current monolith.
3. ECS & Terraform

Containerize + ECS Orchestration + IaC.

  • Pros: Fast setup (under 3 months). 25% cheaper than EKS. Easy horizontal scaling.
  • Cons: Less feature-rich than full K8s (irrelevant for the monolith).
  • Decision: Selected - Optimal balance of speed, cost, and high impact.

The Migration Strategy: Infrastructure as Code First

The migration was executed in logical phases using Terraform as the sole source of truth to ensure consistency and minimize risk, achieving a zero-downtime cutover.

Core Technology Stack & Their "Why"

The chosen tools were simple, reliable, and directly addressed the core metrics of speed, cost, and dependability.

Terraform (IaC Backbone)

Why: Provided a single source of truth, enforced consistency across all environments, and allowed for instant rollbacks, eliminating config drift.

AWS ECS (with Fargate)

Why: Enabled smooth, on-demand auto-scaling to handle 5x traffic spikes without the overhead and cost of full Kubernetes (EKS was 25% more expensive).

AWS RDS & ElastiCache

Why: Managed data and caching, offloading operational work and cutting latency by handling stateful data with built-in replication.

CloudWatch & X-Ray

Why: Centralized logging, metrics, and traces linked directly to SLOs, enabling quick triage and cutting MTTR to under 20 minutes.
